Privacy Policy

How Shehadeh Law Office collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information.

1. Commitment to Privacy

Shehadeh Law Office is committed to protecting the confidentiality and security of personal information submitted through this website or provided during an initial enquiry. This policy explains, in general terms, how that information may be collected, used, stored, and protected.

2. Information We May Collect

We may collect information when you:

  • complete the contact form
  • send us an email
  • communicate with the firm by telephone
  • browse the website and generate standard server logs

That information may include:

  • name and surname
  • email address
  • telephone number
  • postal or location information you provide voluntarily
  • details relevant to your legal enquiry
  • technical browsing information such as IP address and browser type

3. How Information Is Used

Information submitted to the firm may be used to:

  • respond to consultation requests and enquiries
  • evaluate whether the firm can assist with a matter
  • communicate with you regarding your legal issue
  • administer and improve the website
  • comply with legal, ethical, or professional obligations

4. Professional Confidentiality

As a law office, Shehadeh Law Office treats communications with a high degree of professional discretion. However, sending information through the website alone does not automatically create an attorney-client relationship.

"Confidentiality is one of the essential norms of our profession."

5. Disclosure of Information

Personal information is not sold or disclosed casually. It may be disclosed only where necessary, including:

  • with your authorization
  • where required by law, regulation, or court order
  • where reasonably necessary for legal representation or professional administration
  • where service providers are engaged under appropriate confidentiality expectations

6. Data Security

The firm seeks to maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal information against unauthorized access, misuse, alteration, disclosure, or loss. No method of transmission or storage can be guaranteed absolutely secure, but reasonable safeguards are applied.

7. Data Retention

Information is retained only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, for ongoing professional engagement, or for compliance with applicable legal and professional retention requirements.

8. Cookies and Technical Data

This website may rely on standard server logs and limited technical mechanisms, including cookies where applicable, to support normal website operation, performance, and basic analytics. You may adjust cookie preferences through your browser settings.

9. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to request:

  • access to personal information held about you
  • correction of inaccurate information
  • deletion of information where retention is no longer justified
  • restriction or objection to certain processing activities

Requests should be directed to the firm using the contact information below.

10. Policy Updates

Shehadeh Law Office may revise this policy from time to time. Any updated version will be posted on this page and will apply from the date of publication.
